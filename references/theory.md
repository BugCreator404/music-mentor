# Music Theory Reference

Complete reference for teaching music theory at all levels. Load when user asks about
scales, intervals, modes, chords construction, rhythm, or any foundational theory concept.

---

## THE NOTE SYSTEM

### The 12 chromatic notes
```
C  C#/Db  D  D#/Eb  E  F  F#/Gb  G  G#/Ab  A  A#/Bb  B
```
- **Enharmonic equivalents**: C# = Db (same pitch, different name depending on key context)
- **Half step (semitone)**: smallest distance between two adjacent notes
- **Whole step (tone)**: two semitones

### Intervals (distance between two notes)

| Name | ES | Semitones | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unison | Unísono | 0 | C–C |
| Minor 2nd | 2ª menor | 1 | C–Db |
| Major 2nd | 2ª mayor | 2 | C–D |
| Minor 3rd | 3ª menor | 3 | C–Eb |
| Major 3rd | 3ª mayor | 4 | C–E |
| Perfect 4th | 4ª justa | 5 | C–F |
| Tritone (aug 4th / dim 5th) | Tritono | 6 | C–F#/Gb |
| Perfect 5th | 5ª justa | 7 | C–G |
| Minor 6th | 6ª menor | 8 | C–Ab |
| Major 6th | 6ª mayor | 9 | C–A |
| Minor 7th | 7ª menor | 10 | C–Bb |
| Major 7th | 7ª mayor | 11 | C–B |
| Octave | Octava | 12 | C–C |

**Consonant intervals**: Unison, 3rds, 4th, 5th, 6ths, Octave
**Dissonant intervals**: 2nds, 7ths, Tritone (wants to resolve)

## SCALES

### Major Scale formula
**W W H W W W H** (W=whole step, H=half step)

All major scales:
```
C:  C D E F G A B C
G:  G A B C D E F# G
D:  D E F# G A B C# D
A:  A B C# D E F# G# A
E:  E F# G# A B C# D# E
B:  B C# D# E F# G# A# B
F#: F# G# A# B C# D# E# F#
F:  F G A Bb C D E F
Bb: Bb C D Eb F G A Bb
Eb: Eb F G Ab Bb C D Eb
Ab: Ab Bb C Db Eb F G Ab
Db: Db Eb F Gb Ab Bb C Db
```

### Natural Minor Scale formula
**W H W W H W W**

Relative minor = 6th degree of major scale (e.g., Am is relative to C major)

### Harmonic Minor
Natural minor with raised 7th: **W H W W H aug2 H**
Purpose: creates a V7 chord (dominant) → resolves strongly to i

### Melodic Minor
Ascending: **W H W W W W H** (raised 6th and 7th)
Descending: same as natural minor
Purpose: avoids the awkward augmented 2nd in harmonic minor when singing

### Pentatonic Scales
**Major pentatonic**: 1 2 3 5 6 (remove 4th and 7th from major)
**Minor pentatonic**: 1 ♭3 4 5 ♭7 (remove 2nd and 6th from natural minor)

Minor pentatonic in C: C Eb F G Bb
→ Most common scale in rock, blues, pop solos

**Blues scale**: Minor pentatonic + ♭5 (blue note)
C blues: C Eb F Gb G Bb C

## THE 7 MODES

All modes come from playing a major scale starting from a different degree.
Key insight: **modes aren't just scales — they're tonal colors/moods.**

| Mode | Start degree | Formula from C major | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ionian | 1st (C) | C D E F G A B | Bright, happy (= major scale) |
| Dorian | 2nd (D) | D E F G A B C D | Minor but hopeful, jazzy |
| Phrygian | 3rd (E) | E F G A B C D E | Dark, flamenco, Spanish flavor |
| Lydian | 4th (F) | F G A B C D E F | Dreamy, floating, ethereal |
| Mixolydian | 5th (G) | G A B C D E F G | Major but bluesy, rock/folk |
| Aeolian | 6th (A) | A B C D E F G A | Natural minor, melancholic |
| Locrian | 7th (B) | B C D E F G A B | Unstable, rarely used as tonic |

**Teaching tip**: Don't memorize modes as "scale starting on X." Learn them by their
characteristic interval:
- Dorian = minor scale with raised 6th (♮6)
- Phrygian = minor scale with ♭2
- Lydian = major scale with ♯4
- Mixolydian = major scale with ♭7
- Locrian = minor scale with ♭2 and ♭5

## CHORD CONSTRUCTION

### Triads (3-note chords)
Built by stacking 3rds from a root note.

| Quality | Formula | Example | Sound |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major | 1 3 5 | C E G | Bright, stable |
| Minor | 1 ♭3 5 | C Eb G | Dark, melancholic |
| Diminished | 1 ♭3 ♭5 | C Eb Gb | Tense, unstable |
| Augmented | 1 3 ♯5 | C E G# | Dreamy, unresolved |

### 7th Chords (4-note chords)
Add another 3rd on top of the triad.

| Quality | Formula | Symbol | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major 7th | 1 3 5 7 | Cmaj7 | C E G B |
| Dominant 7th | 1 3 5 ♭7 | C7 | C E G Bb |
| Minor 7th | 1 ♭3 5 ♭7 | Cm7 | C Eb G Bb |
| Half-diminished | 1 ♭3 ♭5 ♭7 | Cm7♭5 or Cø | C Eb Gb Bb |
| Diminished 7th | 1 ♭3 ♭5 ♭♭7 | Cdim7 | C Eb Gb A |
| Minor/Major 7th | 1 ♭3 5 7 | CmM7 | C Eb G B |

### Extensions (jazz/advanced)
9th = 2nd an octave up, 11th = 4th an octave up, 13th = 6th an octave up

Cmaj9: C E G B D
C13: C E G Bb D A (dominant 13th — most notes optional except root, 3rd, 7th, 13th)

### Chord inversions
- **Root position**: root on bottom (C E G)
- **1st inversion**: 3rd on bottom (E G C) — symbol: C/E
- **2nd inversion**: 5th on bottom (G C E) — symbol: C/G

## RHYTHM & METER

### Note values
```
Whole note      = 4 beats     ○
Half note       = 2 beats     ♩ (open head + stem)
Quarter note    = 1 beat      ♩
Eighth note     = 1/2 beat    ♪
Sixteenth note  = 1/4 beat    ♬
```
**Dotted note** = note × 1.5 (dotted quarter = 1.5 beats)
**Triplet** = 3 notes in the space of 2 (8th note triplet = 3 notes in 1 beat)

### Time signatures
```
4/4  — 4 beats per bar, quarter note = 1 beat  (most common)
3/4  — 3 beats per bar (waltz feel)
6/8  — 6 eighth notes per bar, felt in 2 (compound duple)
12/8 — 12 eighth notes per bar, felt in 4 (blues, gospel)
5/4  — asymmetric, often felt as 3+2 or 2+3
7/8  — asymmetric, often felt as 3+2+2 or 2+2+3
```

### Subdivisions for practice
Always subdivide internally:
- 4/4 at slow tempo: count "1-and-2-and-3-and-4-and"
- Triplets: "1-trip-let-2-trip-let"
- Sixteenths: "1-e-and-a-2-e-and-a"

## LEVEL PROGRESSION GUIDE

When teaching, follow this sequence:

**Beginner path**:
1. Note names on staff / fretboard / keyboard
2. Major scale (C major first)
3. Basic triads (I, IV, V in C major)
4. Simple rhythm in 4/4
5. First pentatonic scale

**Intermediate path**:
1. All 12 major scales + key signatures
2. Natural/harmonic minor scales
3. All triad qualities
4. 7th chords (maj7, dom7, min7)
5. Modes (Dorian and Mixolydian first — most practical)
6. Compound time signatures (6/8, 12/8)

**Advanced path**:
1. All modes with characteristic intervals
2. Extended chords (9ths, 11ths, 13ths)
3. Harmonic analysis with Roman numerals
4. Secondary dominants and borrowed chords
5. Melodic minor modes (Lydian dominant, Altered scale)
6. Counterpoint basics

**Expert path**:
1. Complete harmonic analysis of complex pieces
2. Tritone substitutions
3. Reharmonization
4. Modal interchange
5. Atonal/12-tone concepts
