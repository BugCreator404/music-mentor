# TAB Examples & Visual Reference

Annotated examples for reading and interpreting guitar/bass tablature.
Reference material for the `notation.md` module.

---

## EXAMPLE 1 — Basic melody (beginner)

```
e |--0--2--3--2--0----|
B |-------------------|
G |-------------------|
D |-------------------|
A |-------------------|
E |-------------------|
```
**What it is**: Simple melody on the high E string, open position.
**Notes**: E – F# – G – F# – E
**Key**: G major (the F# tells us we're not in C major)
**How to play**: Pick each note individually, let them ring.

## EXAMPLE 2 — Power chord riff (beginner/intermediate)

```
e |----------------------|
B |----------------------|
G |----------------------|
D |--5--5--3--3--2--2----|
A |--5--5--3--3--2--2----|
E |--3--3--1--1--0--0----|
```
**What it is**: Power chord progression (two strings fretted together)
**Chords**: G5 – F5 – E5
**Key context**: Natural minor / rock context
**How to play**: Bar two strings per chord with one finger, palm mute optional.

## EXAMPLE 3 — Techniques (intermediate)

```
e |--------------------------------|
B |--------------------------------|
G |--7h9--9p7--7b9--7b9r7--7~-----|
D |--------------------------------|
A |--------------------------------|
E |--------------------------------|
```
**What it is**: Blues/rock lick using common techniques
**Breakdown**:
- `7h9` → fret 7, hammer-on to 9 (one pick stroke)
- `9p7` → fret 9, pull-off to 7 (one pick stroke)
- `7b9` → fret 7, bend string until it sounds like fret 9
- `7b9r7` → bend to 9, then release back to 7
- `7~` → fret 7 with vibrato (oscillate pitch)

**Scale**: A minor pentatonic (G string, position 2)

## EXAMPLE 4 — Arpeggio pattern (intermediate)

```
e |--0-----------0-----------0---------|
B |------1-----------1-----------1-----|
G |----------0-----------0-----------0-|
D |------------------------------------|
A |--0---------------------------------|
E |------------------------------------|
```
**What it is**: Fingerpicked Am arpeggio
**Chord**: Am (x02210) — only top 3 strings + open A bass
**Pattern**: Bass note → B string → high e → repeat
**Technique**: Use thumb for A string, index for B, middle for e (or pick with alternating motion)

## EXAMPLE 5 — 12-bar blues riff (intermediate)

```
E7 (I):
e |-------------------------------|
B |-------------------------------|
G |-------------------------------|
D |--2--2--4--4--2--2--4--4-------|
A |--2--2--4--4--2--2--4--4-------|
E |--0--0--0--0--0--0--0--0-------|

A7 (IV):
e |-------------------------------|
B |-------------------------------|
G |-------------------------------|
D |--2--2--4--4--2--2--4--4-------|
A |--0--0--0--0--0--0--0--0-------|
E |-------------------------------|

B7 (V):
e |-------------------------------|
B |-------------------------------|
G |-------------------------------|
D |--4--4--6--6--4--4--6--6-------|
A |--2--2--2--2--2--2--2--2-------|
E |-------------------------------|
```
**What it is**: Shuffle blues riff in E, covers all three chords of a 12-bar blues
**Key**: E major/blues
**Rhythm**: Play with a shuffle feel — long-short, long-short (swing 8ths)

## EXAMPLE 6 — Bass TAB (beginner)

```
G |-------------------------------|
D |-------------------------------|
A |--3--3--3--3--5--5--5--5-------|
E |--1--1--1--1--3--3--3--3-------|
```
**What it is**: Walking bass line outlining F and G chords
**Notes**: F root (E string fret 1) and C (A string fret 3) → G root (fret 3) and D (fret 5)
**Pattern**: Root + 5th alternating = classic bass pattern
**Rhythm**: Quarter notes, one per beat in 4/4

## CHORD DIAGRAM REFERENCE

Standard chord shapes (guitar, EADGBE tuning):

```
      E A D G B e          E A D G B e
Em:   0 2 2 0 0 0    Am:   x 0 2 2 1 0
Dm:   x x 0 2 3 1    C:    x 3 2 0 1 0
G:    3 2 0 0 3 3    F:    1 3 3 2 1 1  ← barre
A:    x 0 2 2 2 0    E:    0 2 2 1 0 0
D:    x x 0 2 3 2    B7:   x 2 1 2 0 2

Barre chord shapes (moveable):
Major barre (E shape, root on E string):
   x x x x x x
   [root fret]
   1 1 1 1 1 1   ← index finger barres all strings
   . 3 . . . .
   . . 4 . . .
   . . . . . .

Minor barre (Em shape):
   1 1 1 1 1 1
   . . . . . .
   . 3 . . . .
   . . 4 . . .
```

## TAB SYMBOLS QUICK REFERENCE

```
h    = hammer-on          5h7   = pick 5, hammer to 7
p    = pull-off           7p5   = pick 7, pull to 5
b    = bend               7b9   = bend 7 up to sound like 9
r    = release bend       7b9r7 = bend up, release back
/    = slide up           5/7   = slide from 5 to 7
\    = slide down         7\5   = slide from 7 to 5
~    = vibrato            7~    = vibrato on fret 7
x    = muted note         x     = percussive thud
t    = tap (right hand)   t12   = tap fret 12
<n>  = harmonic           <12>  = natural harmonic at fret 12
PM-  = palm mute          PM--- = palm muted notes
^    = pick scrape
```
